The Mission
Each team builds one or more functional AI agents and submits a complete project proposal. 
To help you get started, a catalog of suggested agents has been put together covering the four strategic domains. 
It's a source of inspiration, not a set menu. Build one from the list, combine several, or you can propose something completely new. 
What you build should be something that a SOC analyst, incident responder, or security engineer would genuinely use — 
and do it well enough to be able to demonstrate it. The catalog spans 4 strategic domains, 
organized around where the need is greatest and where AI-powered defense can make the biggest difference:
•	Central Infrastructure
•	SOC and Detection
•	Computer Forensics and Incident Response
•	Application and Cloud Security
•	Newly Identified Gaps
The strongest proposals won't just classify—reason, contextualize, and explain.

Open Source as a Requirement
Every presentation should be built on an open source model as a foundation. 
Teams are free to adjust, extend, expand, 
and redesign that foundation based on what their strategy demands, 
but tracking must be traced back to an openly available model.
